How to Use Our App — Quick Guide

Plan Your Route

Enter your starting point and destination based on your user type, such as wheelchair user or elder. The app will highlight suitable accessible routes and display relevant facilities along the way — like ramps, crosswalks, and resting areas — to help you make an informed decision.

Save Your Favorite Routes

Once you've found a route that fits your needs, you can bookmark it to your favorites. These saved routes can later be accessed and managed easily from your Favorites section in the dashboard.

Add and View Accessibility POIs

You can create your own Points of Interest (POIs) directly on the map — such as elevators, ramps, or benches. Once added, these POIs will appear in your personal Dashboard → Places list. You can also edit or delete your contributed POIs anytime from the Profile page.

Share a Post with the Community

On the Dashboard page, click the plus (+) button to create a new post — whether it’s a helpful tip, a location recommendation, or a barrier warning. Once published, your post will automatically appear in the Community section on the homepage for others to see and interact with.

Stay Connected in the Community

Your Dashboard is the central hub for staying up to date. It includes:

  • Community — View recent posts shared by users.
  • Places — View all the user POIs with location data.
  • Announcements — Get system alerts about app updates or nearby emergencies.

You can like or dislike on posts to interact with others and stay informed about the world around you.

About Us

Our team BBY21 at BCIT is developing PathPal, a responsive web and mobile application that empowers wheelchair users, seniors, caregivers, and allies to navigate urban spaces with confidence. By integrating OpenStreetMap data and crowd-sourced contributions, PathPal highlights key accessibility features—ramps, benches, restrooms, and gentle-incline paths—while offering customizable filters for wheelchair- or senior-friendly routes. Users can upload photos, vote on entries to validate accuracy, and export GPX files for offline navigation. With a gamified rewards system—points, badges, and levels—we’ll foster an engaged community that continually enriches the map’s coverage and reliability.
